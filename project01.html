Murdock From Paragon

— Steampunk style modle making

Steampunk

- Sniper Shotgun & Mech Arm

For this project, I have made two steampunk costume pieces. The mech arm gear and the sniper shotgun. The idea comes from a skin concept art of Murdock from the game Paragon.

One of the reason why I want to go for a steampunk style costume is that we believe steampunk is an art of detail. It is a style that people will find the beauty within the details of creation.

Mech Arm Anotation

For the steampunk mech arm, I used the 7mm EVA foam floor mat as the main material for the 3 major pieces (Upper shoulder, arm pad, and arm). then use a 2mm EVA foam sheet for decoration (e.g. Detailed gear, hydraulic pump, battery, etc.).

I also use a hole puncher to punch holes and connect them with 3mm wires. In order to connect all three pieces together, I need to make a nut and bolt with ¼ inch vinyl tube. I use the head of the hot glue gun and reshape the head of the vinyl tube in order to lock down the piece while allowing them to rotate.

Sniper Gun Anotation

I used ½ inch PEX pipe and roll it with aluminum tape to create the gun barrels. The gun handle is created with a pink foam core board and sand it into the desired shape. On top of the handle, we made decorations with the 2mm foam sheet. We made the scope out of magazine paper and roll it with aluminum tape.

I also 3D printed the shotgun round holder and the trigger. The shotgun rounds I also used ½ inch PEX pipe and roll it with aluminum tape. After all the pieces are assembled, we painted the whole gun with Metallic black paint as a base and apply the metallic paint on top of the base color.

Meterial

The project use different materials. For example, I use sandpaper to sand the form to the manipulation of pink foam. This material allow me to create an organic shape. I use the pink foam to create the main supporting parts of the sniper shotgun. I use hot up the glue gun to glue the pieces that we need. I have also used aluminum tape to fake the PVC tube into a metal rod and 3D printer to make a bullet holder.

Process

I have used gold, bronze silver metallic paint for detail painting after the black coating is dried out. One of the challenges that I have met is that the paints might be too limited in terms of variety. So I decided to mix the paint and create some marks of age on top of the other paints. I have learned that it is a good thing to paint after the assembly of all the parts. The project also taught me lots of handcraft skills and good painting skills.
